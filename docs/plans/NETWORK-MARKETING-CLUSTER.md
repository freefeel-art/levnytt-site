## 1. Cluster Mission

### Why This Cluster Exists

Network marketing (MLM, direktförsäljning) is one of the most misunderstood business models in Sweden. Public discourse alternates between uncritical hype from recruiters and blanket condemnation from critics. The truth is more nuanced — but nuanced information is hard to find.

Most content about network marketing online serves one of two purposes:
- **Recruitment** — persuasive material designed to sign people up
- **Debunking** — content designed to warn people away

Neither helps someone who simply wants to **understand** the model so they can make their own decision.

This cluster fills that gap. It provides Sweden's most comprehensive, balanced educational resource on network marketing — explaining how the model works, where the criticism comes from, what the research actually says, and how a reader can evaluate any opportunity for themselves.

**This is not a sales section.** No registrations. No recruitment. No earnings claims.

### Who It Serves

| Audience | Their Question | Our Role |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| Consumers who received an MLM pitch | "Is this legit or a scam?" | Provide the evaluation framework |
| People considering joining an MLM | "What am I getting into?" | Explain the model, risks, and realities |
| Friends/family of MLM participants | "How do I help them see the truth?" | Offer evidence-based perspective |
| Students/researchers | "What does the literature say?" | Curate and cite primary sources |
| Journalists | "What are the facts about this industry?" | Provide reference-quality data |
| MLM critics | "What distinguishes legal from illegal?" | Clarify the legal boundary |
| MLM participants seeking context | "Where does my company fit?" | Offer industry-level perspective |
| Policy/regulatory audience | "How is this regulated in Sweden?" | Document legal framework |

### Which Questions It Answers

This cluster exists to answer every major question a Swedish-speaking person might have about network marketing:

**Foundation:**
- What is network marketing and how does it differ from regular direct selling?
- How do MLM compensation plans actually work?
- What is the history of this business model?

**Legal & Regulatory:**
- Is MLM legal in Sweden?
- What is the legal difference between MLM and pyramid schemes?
- How does Swedish law (Marknadsföringslagen) regulate MLM?
- What role does Konsumentverket play?
- What is the Amway 1979 ruling and why does it matter?
- How does EU law apply?

**Criticism & Research:**
- What does FTC research say about MLM incomes?
- Why do most MLM participants earn little or nothing?
- What does independent academic research conclude?
- Is MLM research reliable, or is much of it industry-funded?
- What are the most common criticisms of the model?

**Myths & Misconceptions:**
- "MLM is a pyramid scheme" — is that true?
- "You can get rich in MLM" — what are the real numbers?
- "MLM products are overpriced" — how does pricing work?
- "Only losers fail in MLM" — what does failure actually mean?
- "MLM preys on vulnerable people" — what does evidence show?

**Decision Support:**
- How do I evaluate if an MLM opportunity is right for me?
- What should I look for in an income disclosure statement?
- What questions should I ask before joining?
- What are the warning signs of a bad opportunity?
- How do I compare MLM vs traditional business vs side hustle?

**Industry Landscape:**
- Which MLM/direct sales companies are active in Sweden?
- How do different companies' models compare?
- What does the global direct selling industry look like?
- How is the industry changing with e-commerce and social media?

**NeoLife in Context:**
- Where does NeoLife fit in the broader MLM landscape?
- What distinguishes NeoLife's model from other companies?
- How does NeoLife compare on product quality, pricing, and ethics?

4. **Cross-topic links** — where topics naturally intersect. Examples:
   - A10 (research) links to A11 (criticism review) and A13 (research reliability)

## 5. Editorial Principles

This cluster operates under the LevNytt editorial philosophy:

> **Fakta före hype. Värde före pris. Förstå först. Bestäm sedan.**

### 5.1 What This Cluster Must Do

| Principle | How We Apply It |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Explain** | Every article starts from the assumption that the reader knows nothing about the topic. Define terms. Show mechanisms. Provide context. |
| **Compare** | Never present MLM in isolation. Compare to retail, e-commerce, traditional business, and other models. Let the reader judge. |
| **Analyse** | Include primary-source data. Cite FTC, WFDSA, peer-reviewed research, Swedish law. Attribute everything. |
| **Correct myths** | Address misconceptions directly and respectfully. Show the evidence both for and against common beliefs. |
| **Be transparent** | Disclose LevNytt's relationship to NeoLife (independent distributor) on every article in the cluster. Distinguish between objective analysis and potential bias. |
| **Serve the reader's decision** | End every article with "what this means for you" not "what you should do". Leave the door open, don't push through it. |

### 5.2 What This Cluster Must Never Become

| Prohibited | Why |
|------------|-----|
| Recruitment material | This is education, not persuasion. No "join now" CTAs on informational articles. |
| Hype | No superlatives about income potential, lifestyle, or opportunity. |
| "Get rich quick" framing | No income claims. No "passive income" language. No freedom narratives. |
| Condemnation | Not an anti-MLM crusade. Present evidence, don't moralise. |
| Fear-based warnings | "Don't be scammed" is acceptable if evidence-based. Panic language is not. |
| Unsourced claims | Every factual claim must have a verifiable source. Tier 1-2 preferred. |
| Anecdote as evidence | Individual success stories and failure stories are illustrative, never conclusive. |
| False balance | When evidence clearly supports one side (e.g., FTC findings on median income), don't manufacture false equivalence. |

### 5.3 Tone Guide

| Quality | Target | Avoid |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Register | Professional, accessible (lättläst sakprosa) | Academic jargon, slang |
| Stance | Curious, investigative, explanatory | Certain, preachy, dismissive |
| Level of detail | Thorough but structured | Dense walls of text |
| Transparency | Explicit about bias and affiliation | Hidden agenda, assumed authority |
| Reader relationship | Guide, not gatekeeper | Salesperson, not judge |

### 5.4 Source Hierarchy

Apply the evidence-tiering system used elsewhere on LevNytt:

| Tier | Type | Examples |
|------|------|----------|
| Tier 1 | Meta-analyses, systematic reviews, regulatory rulings | FTC report 2024, Groß & Keep 2025 |
| Tier 2 | Primary research, government data | Bosley 2021, WFDSA statistics, Konsumentverket |
| Tier 3 | Industry data, company disclosures | Income disclosure statements, company reports |
| Tier 4 | Expert opinion, legal commentary | Legal analyses, regulatory guidance |

Tier 4 sources must be flagged. Tier 3 data from MLM companies must be contextualised (e.g., "enligt företagets egna siffror...").

### 5.5 Disclosure Requirements

Every article in the cluster must include a transparency statement, adapted to context:

Denna artikel är en del av ett konsumentutbildningskluster om nätverksmarknadsföring — inte rekryteringsmaterial. Alla påståenden citeras från angivna källor.
