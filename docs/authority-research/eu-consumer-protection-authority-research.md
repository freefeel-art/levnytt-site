## 1. Topic

The **European Union consumer-protection framework** sets minimum standards that member states must implement in national law. For direct selling and network marketing, the most relevant instruments are the **Consumer Rights Directive**, the **Unfair Commercial Practices Directive**, and related rules on food supplements, health claims, and e-commerce.

### Core definitions

| Term | Definition | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Consumer Rights Directive (CRD) 2011/83/EU** | EU directive governing distance and off-premises contracts. | Requires pre-contract information and 14-day withdrawal right. |
| **Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (UCPD) 2005/29/EC** | Prohibits misleading and aggressive commercial practices. | Directly applicable to income claims and recruitment pressure. |
| **Distance contract** | Contract concluded without simultaneous physical presence of seller and consumer. | Applies to online, phone, and catalogue sales typical in direct selling. |
| **Off-premises contract** | Contract concluded away from business premises (e.g., consumer's home, party plan). | Direct selling is often off-premises. |
| **Withdrawal / cooling-off right** | Consumer's right to cancel within 14 days without justification. | Applies to most distance and off-premises contracts. |
| **Misleading commercial practice** | Practice that deceives or is likely to deceive consumers and affects their economic behaviour. | Includes misleading income claims and earnings testimonials. |
| **Health claim** | Claim that a food/product has a health benefit. | Regulated separately; relevant for supplement-based MLMs. |

## 3. Tier 0 original sources

### EU legislation

- **Directive 2011/83/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council of 25 October 2011 on consumer rights.**
  - **Type:** EU directive
  - **Importance:** Consolidates and updates earlier directives on distance and door-step selling; gives 14-day withdrawal right.

- **Directive 2005/29/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 11 May 2005 concerning unfair business-to-consumer commercial practices.**
  - **Type:** EU directive
  - **Importance:** Prohibits misleading actions, misleading omissions, and aggressive practices; directly applies to MLM income claims.

- **Council Directive 85/577/EEC (now integrated into CRD).** *To protect the consumer in respect of contracts negotiated away from business premises.*
  - **Type:** EU directive (repealed by CRD but historically important)
  - **Importance:** Established early EU cooling-off rights for door-to-door/direct selling.

- **Regulation (EC) No 1924/2006 on nutrition and health claims made on foods.**
  - **Type:** EU regulation directly applicable
  - **Importance:** Governs health claims for foods and food supplements, including those sold by MLMs.

- **Regulation (EU) 2019/1150 on promoting fairness and transparency for business users of online intermediation services (Platform-to-Business Regulation).**
  - **Type:** EU regulation
  - **Importance:** Increasingly relevant as MLM recruitment and sales move to social media and platform-based tools.

### European Commission guidance

- **European Commission Staff Working Document on UCPD Guidance (2021 update and earlier versions).**
  - **Type:** Official guidance
  - **Importance:** Explains how UCPD applies to pyramid schemes, misleading commercial practices, and digital environments.

- **European Commission / DG JUST consumer-protection webpages.**
  - **Type:** Official guidance
  - **Importance:** Practical summaries of consumer rights for EU citizens.

### Industry / trade

- **Seldia (European Direct Selling Association).** Position papers on EU direct selling regulation.
  - **Type:** Industry advocacy documents
  - **Importance:** Industry perspective on how EU law should apply to direct selling.

- **WFDSA / European market data.**
  - **Type:** Industry statistics

## 5. Major research, reviews and regulatory decisions

### Consumer Rights Directive (2011/83/EU)

Key provisions relevant to direct selling/MLM:
- **Pre-contractual information:** Sellers must provide identity, address, price, main characteristics, right of withdrawal, etc.
- **14-day withdrawal right:** Consumers may cancel distance and off-premises contracts within 14 days without giving a reason.
- **Model withdrawal form:** Sellers must provide or make available a standard cancellation form.
- **Refund timing:** Refunds must generally be provided within 14 days.
- **Exceptions:** Some contracts (e.g., personalised goods, perishables) are excluded; most physical product sales by MLM are covered.

### Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (2005/29/EC)

Key provisions:
- **Misleading actions:** Deceptive or likely-to-deceive practices concerning existence/nature of product, price, need for service, etc.
- **Misleading omissions:** Omission of material information that consumers need to make informed decisions.
- **Aggressive practices:** Harassment, coercion, or undue influence that impairs consumer freedom of choice.
- **Black list (Annex I):** Prohibited practices including bait advertising, pyramid promotional schemes (No. 14: "Establishing, operating or promoting a pyramid promotional scheme where a consumer gives consideration for the opportunity to receive compensation that is derived primarily from the introduction of other consumers into the scheme rather than from the sale or consumption of products").
- **Country of origin principle:** Businesses must comply with consumer-protection rules of the consumer's country of habitual residence.

### Health-claim regulation (Regulation 1924/2006)

- Only health claims listed in the EU Register or authorised by EFSA can be used.
- Claims must not be false, ambiguous, or misleading.
- **Relevance to MLM:** Distributors making unauthorised health claims on social media can expose themselves and the company to enforcement action.

### Implementation in Sweden

- Consumer Rights Directive implemented in **Lag (2005:59) om distansavtal och avtal utanför affärslokaler**.
- UCPD implemented in **Marknadsföringslagen (2008:486)**.
- Health claims regulation is directly applicable as EU regulation.

## 7. Established scientific / regulatory consensus

1. **EU law provides a harmonised baseline for consumer protection in direct selling.** Key directives are the CRD and UCPD.
2. **Pyramid promotional schemes are expressly prohibited under the UCPD black list.** This applies in all member states.
3. **Consumers have a 14-day cooling-off right for distance and off-premises contracts,** including most direct-sales purchases.
4. **Misleading income claims, omissions of material information, and aggressive recruitment practices violate EU unfair-commercial-practices law.**
5. **Health claims for foods and supplements must be authorised and listed in the EU Register.** Distributors are not exempt from these rules.
6. **National authorities enforce EU consumer law.** In Sweden, Konsumentverket and the Consumer Ombudsman play central roles.
7. **Cross-border enforcement is coordinated through the CPC-Network and ECC-Net.** Consumers can seek help with foreign sellers.
8. **EU law does not ban MLM or direct selling as such.** It prohibits misleading practices and pyramid schemes within those models.
9. **Industry self-regulation (e.g., Seldia codes) complements but does not replace EU and national law.**
10. **Digital direct selling and social-media recruitment create new enforcement challenges** not fully addressed by 2005-era directives.

## 8. Areas of controversy

1. **Application of UCPD to social-media income testimonials.** Modern marketing often bypasses traditional commercial-practices concepts.
2. **Distributor liability for health claims.** Whether individual distributors or only the company are liable for unauthorised claims is debated and fact-specific.
3. **Non-EU MLMs selling to EU consumers.** Jurisdiction and enforcement against companies based outside the EU can be difficult.
4. **Harmonisation vs national variation.** Member states may have stricter rules; MLMs must comply with the law of the consumer's country.
5. **Whether the UCPD black list is adequately enforced.** Pyramid-scheme prohibition exists but enforcement resources vary.
6. ** cooling-off right for digital products and subscriptions.** MLMs with auto-ship or digital-training subscriptions may face complex rules.
7. **Greenwashing and sustainability claims.** New rules (e.g., 2022 amendment to Swedish Marketing Act / EU Green Claims Directive proposal) increasingly affect product marketing.
8. **Influencer/affiliate marketing overlap.** Distributors promoting products online may be subject to advertising-disclosure requirements.

## 9. Common misconceptions

| Misconception | Correction / nuance |
|---|---|
| "EU law bans all MLM." | The EU bans pyramid schemes and misleading practices; legitimate direct selling/MLM remains legal. |
| "The 14-day cooling-off right applies to everything." | Applies to distance and off-premises contracts; some exceptions exist. |
| "Health claims by distributors are not regulated." | Unauthorised health claims for food/supplements violate EU regulation, regardless of who makes them. |
| "Only the company, not the distributor, is liable for marketing claims." | Distributors can also be liable depending on jurisdiction and facts. |
| "If a company is legal in one EU country, it is legal in all." | Companies must comply with the consumer-protection rules of each member state where they operate. |
| "EU directives are directly enforceable against companies." | Many directives require national implementation; enforcement is primarily national. |
| "Pyramid schemes are not covered by EU law." | UCPD Annex I expressly prohibits pyramid promotional schemes. |
| "Consumer rights do not apply to business opportunities." | Many direct-sales purchases are consumer contracts covered by the CRD and UCPD. |

## Factual consistency notes

- **EU directives are transposed into national law.** Swedish readers should also consult Swedish law and Konsumentverket guidance.
- **Pyramid promotional schemes are explicitly prohibited by UCPD Annex I.** This is a hard prohibition, not merely guidance.
- **Cooling-off rights apply to most distance and off-premises contracts** but have exceptions; verify before making unqualified statements.
- **Health-claim rules apply equally to MLM products** and retail products; the distribution channel does not create exemptions.
- **National enforcement varies.** EU law provides a floor; some member states may be stricter.
- **Distinguish between EU directives (implemented nationally) and EU regulations (directly applicable).** Health-claim regulation is a regulation; CRD and UCPD are directives.
