## 1. Topic

**Direct selling** is the sale of goods or services to consumers away from fixed retail locations, typically through person-to-person contact, demonstrations, home parties, catalogues, or digital channels managed by independent sellers. It is a recognised distribution channel that predates modern retail and includes both single-level sales and multi-level compensation structures.

### Core definitions

| Term | Definition | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct selling** | Sale of products/services away from permanent retail premises, through independent sellers. | Broad umbrella covering door-to-door, party-plan, catalogue, and MLM. |
| **Single-level direct selling** | Sellers earn income only from their own sales, with no recruitment-based compensation. | Legal everywhere direct sales are permitted. |
| **Party-plan selling** | Products demonstrated and sold in group settings, usually in a host's home. | Popular for cosmetics, kitchenware, and household goods. |
| **Network marketing / MLM** | Direct selling in which sellers may also earn from the sales of recruited distributors. | Legally defined by the product-sales test. |
| **Direct sales company** | A company that distributes products primarily through independent sellers rather than retail stores. | May be single-level or multi-level. |

## 3. Tier 0 original sources

### Regulatory / legal / government

- **U.S. Federal Trade Commission (FTC). Business Opportunity Rule (2012).**
  - **Type:** Federal rule
  - **Importance:** Requires disclosure of key information for business-opportunity sellers, including litigation history, earnings claims, and cancellation/refund policies. Applies to many direct-selling opportunities in the U.S.

- **Council Directive 85/577/EEC (now integrated into Consumer Rights Directive 2011/83/EU).** *Contracts negotiated away from business premises.*
  - **Type:** EU legislation
  - **Importance:** Established cooling-off rights for off-premises contracts including direct sales.

- **Directive 2011/83/EU.** *Consumer Rights Directive.*
  - **Type:** EU legislation
  - **Importance:** Governs distance and off-premises contracts, including withdrawal rights, information requirements, and cooling-off periods across the EU.

- **Directive 2005/29/EC.** *Unfair Commercial Practices Directive.*
  - **Type:** EU legislation
  - **Importance:** Prohibits misleading and aggressive commercial practices, including misleading income claims.

- **Marknadsföringslagen (2008:486).** *Swedish Marketing Act.*
  - **Type:** National legislation
  - **Importance:** Implements UCPD and governs misleading marketing, including claims made by direct sellers and MLMs.

- **Lag (2005:59) om distansavtal och avtal utanför affärslokaler.** *Swedish Distance and Doorstep Sales Act.*
  - **Type:** National legislation
  - **Importance:** Implements the Consumer Rights Directive in Sweden; gives consumers 14-day cooling-off right for distance and off-premises sales.

### Industry / trade association

- **World Federation of Direct Selling Associations (WFDSA). 2024 Global STATS Report.**
  - **Type:** Original industry statistics
  - **Importance:** Primary source for global direct selling retail sales ($163.9 billion), independent contractors (104.3 million), and female participation (72.1%).

- **WFDSA. Code of Ethics.**
  - **Type:** Industry self-regulation
  - **Importance:** Sets claimed minimum standards for member companies, including buy-back policies and earnings-claim accuracy.

- **Direct Selling Association (DSA-US). Code of Ethics and membership requirements.**
  - **Type:** National trade-association self-regulation
  - **Importance:** U.S. national companion to WFDSA; includes specific membership and ethics criteria.

### Peer-reviewed / independent research

- **Bosley, Stacey (2021).** *Multi-level marketing income disclosure statements.*
  - **Type:** Peer-reviewed research
  - **Importance:** Estimate that ~25% of U.S. MLM sellers profit; relevant because many direct-selling companies use MLM compensation.

- **Groß & Keep (2025).** *Review of direct selling / MLM research bias.* Journal of Marketing Management.
  - **Type:** Peer-reviewed review
  - **Importance:** Found 59% of reviewed articles had industry ties and downplayed risks.

## 5. Major research, reviews and regulatory decisions

### Legislation and guidelines

- **FTC Business Opportunity Rule (2012)**
  - Requires sellers of business opportunities (including some direct-selling opportunities) to provide a disclosure document covering litigation, earnings claims, and refund terms.
  - Does not fully cover all MLMs because some compensation models fall outside the rule's narrow definition.

- **EU Consumer Rights Directive (2011/83/EU)**
  - Mandates 14-day cooling-off right for distance and off-premises contracts.
  - Requires clear pre-contractual information.

- **EU Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (2005/29/EC)**
  - Prohibits misleading omissions, misleading actions, and aggressive practices.
  - Directly relevant to income claims and recruitment pressure.

### Landmark cases relevant to direct selling

- **FTC v. Amway (1979)**
  - Established that multi-level direct selling is legal if it includes safeguards ensuring income derives from retail sales.

- **FTC v. Herbalife (2016)**
  - Major settlement showing that direct-selling/MLM practices can cross into illegal pyramid-scheme territory if compensation is recruitment-driven.

- **FTC v. BurnLounge (2015)**
  - Ninth Circuit affirmed that BurnLounge was a pyramid scheme despite sales of music products; clarified the primacy of the "ultimate user" test.

### Industry research

- **WFDSA 2024 Global STATS**
  - $163.9 billion global retail sales.
  - 104.3 million independent contractors globally.
  - 72.1% female.
  - Leading regions: Asia-Pacific, Americas, Europe.
  - Leading sectors: wellness, cosmetics & personal care, household goods.

## 7. Established scientific / regulatory consensus

1. **Direct selling is a legitimate distribution channel** when products or services are sold to genuine end users and compensation is tied primarily to sales.
2. **Not all direct selling is MLM.** Single-level and party-plan selling differ from multi-level compensation models.
3. **The legal boundary with pyramid schemes depends on the source of compensation.** Recruitment-driven compensation, inventory loading, and lack of genuine retail customers are warning signs.
4. **Consumers in direct selling transactions have cooling-off rights** under EU and Swedish law for distance and off-premises contracts.
5. **Income claims in direct selling must be truthful and not misleading.** This applies across U.S., EU, and Swedish marketing law.
6. **Industry statistics describe sales and participation scale, not distributor profitability.** WFDSA figures are useful for industry scale but cannot be used to imply typical earnings.
7. **Self-regulation by DSAs complements but does not replace government enforcement.** Code-of-ethics membership is not a guarantee of consumer safety or profitability.
8. **Direct selling is often dominated by wellness, cosmetics, and household products.** This sector concentration shapes consumer perceptions and research focus.
9. **Peer-reviewed research on direct selling is sparse and may be industry-influenced.** Findings should be scrutinised for conflicts of interest.

## 8. Areas of controversy

1. **Where does single-level direct selling end and MLM begin?** The boundary is legally clear in principle but can be blurred by compensation designs that reward recruitment indirectly.
2. **Are DSAs effective regulators or industry lobbyists?** Trade associations publish ethics codes but also defend the industry; critics argue self-regulation is weak.
3. **How should "personal use" by distributors be classified?** Internal consumption by distributors can be legitimate retail demand or disguised inventory loading, depending on facts.
4. **Do direct-selling products offer fair value?** Premium pricing is common; independent comparisons are scarce.
5. **What is the social cost of aggressive recruitment?** Social pressure and relationship strain are widely discussed but understudied academically.
6. **Should there be stricter earnings-claim regulation?** FTC and consumer advocates argue current disclosures are inadequate; industry argues self-regulation is sufficient.
7. **How does digitalisation change direct selling?** Social-media selling and influencer recruitment challenge 20th-century regulatory frameworks.

## 9. Common misconceptions

| Misconception | Correction / nuance |
|---|---|
| "Direct selling is always MLM." | Direct selling includes single-level, party-plan, and other non-MLM forms. |
| "Direct selling is illegal." | Direct selling is legal in most jurisdictions; pyramid schemes are illegal. |
| "All direct-selling companies are pyramid schemes." | The legal distinction depends on whether compensation derives from retail sales or recruitment. |
| "If a company is a DSA member, it is guaranteed ethical." | DSA membership indicates adherence to an industry code, not government certification or guarantee of profitability. |
| "Direct selling is a risk-free way to start a business." | Costs, time investment, and income risk vary; most participants earn little. |
| "Direct selling provides passive income." | Income typically requires ongoing active sales and/or recruitment. |
| "Products sold through direct selling are always premium quality." | Quality varies; the distribution channel does not determine product quality. |

## Factual consistency notes

- **Direct selling is a broad distribution channel, not synonymous with MLM.** Future articles must distinguish single-level, party-plan, and multi-level forms.
- **WFDSA figures describe revenue and participation, not earnings.** Avoid implying that $163.9 billion in sales means distributors are profitable.
- **DSA membership / ethics codes are not government approval.** Distinguish self-regulation from legal compliance.
- **EU and Swedish cooling-off rights apply to many direct-sales contracts** but exceptions exist; verify before giving legal advice.
- **"Direct sales" and "MLM" overlap** but the legal risk profile differs; do not treat all direct selling as equally risky.
